Publications

An analysis of the balance sheet of bankrupt companies.

The research evaluates the patrimony structure through the evaluation and depreciation of assets and liabilities of failed businesses. The results show a higher depreciation of assets than liabilities, in liquidation of bankruptcy company's. Therefore, before the bankruptcy, the management’s decision-making in the company during the financial crisis can be considered as an option of maximizing its utility. In an ex-post bankruptcy period, the value of the available assets to bear the liability is lower than in an ex-post time, which may indicate that the manager-owner "sought to guarantee" their gain before the company go to bankruptcy.
